## 3. System Design: CI Job Scheduler

Your CI system receives hundreds of build jobs per day across a monorepo. Each job has dependencies — a test suite can't run until compilation finishes, compilation can't start until linting passes. You have a fleet of build agents to execute these jobs.

Design a CI job scheduler that executes jobs in dependency order, maximizes parallelism across your agent fleet, and handles failures gracefully.

---

### Requirements to clarify / consider

- How do you represent the jobs and their dependencies?
- How do you determine which jobs are ready to run at any given moment?
- How do you assign jobs to agents, and what happens when an agent goes down mid-job?
- If a job fails, which downstream jobs should be cancelled vs. allowed to continue?
- How do you prevent a single large pipeline from starving other pipelines waiting for agents?
- How do you handle jobs that are much slower than others (stragglers)?

---

### Scale

- 500 pipelines/day, each with 10–100 jobs
- Fleet of 50 build agents
- Jobs range from 5 seconds (linting) to 20 minutes (integration tests)
- Agents can fail or become unreachable at any time

---

### Follow-ups an interviewer will likely ask

1. How does your scheduler decide which ready job to run next when there are more ready jobs than available agents?
2. A job has been running for 3x its historical average. What do you do?
3. How do you avoid one large pipeline monopolizing all 50 agents and starving smaller pipelines?
4. An agent dies mid-job. How does the scheduler detect this and recover?
5. How would you make the scheduler itself highly available — what happens if it crashes?
6. How would you give developers visibility into where their pipeline is stuck?
